# Video Shot List — Gemma 4 Mycelium

3:00 max. YouTube unlisted, link in Kaggle writeup.

**Rubric reminder**: judges weight Impact & Vision (40) + Video Pitch & Storytelling (30) = 70 of 100. The video carries this submission.

**Story spine**: a real grower, in a real room, with no signal, gets a real answer from a model that fits on the laptop in front of him. Built on Gemma.

---

## Pacing target

180 seconds total. 5 acts. Long-ish shots (8–12 sec) for cinematic feel; cut to 3–5 sec only inside the demo to keep energy.

| t | act | function |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 – 0:25 | cold open | establish the grower + the stakes |
| 0:25 – 0:55 | the gap | why current AI fails this user |
| 0:55 – 2:00 | the demo | offline laptop, real model, real fix |
| 2:00 – 2:30 | the model | Crowe Logic + Gemma attribution beat |
| 2:30 – 3:00 | the vision | scale + close |

## Music

- Substrate album works thematically and you own it. Pick a track without sung vocals so the VO sits on top — a Talon Engine instrumental from the Substrate companion sessions would be cleanest.
- Music ducks under voice in every act except 0:55–1:55 where the demo audio carries it.

## Subtitles

- Burn in English subtitles. Judges may watch without sound.
- Auto-translate is fine for YouTube but burned-in English matters for the writeup link preview.

## Filming notes

- Use the OM-1 / iPhone you already use for SWM YouTube. No new gear needed.
- Light the grow room with the work lights already there; don't dress it. Authenticity is the point.
- For the screen capture, use macOS's built-in screen recorder at 1080p, 60fps. Avoid OBS overlays.
- Per [[feedback-no-ai-avatar-video]]: real footage of Michael, not synthetic. Lip-sync on real Michael is fine; AI-generated visuals are not.
- Per [[feedback-no-tech-stack]]: do NOT show CroweLM Talon screens, NVIDIA NIM endpoints, Azure references. Keep the tech surface to: the CLI, Gemma attribution, Hugging Face page.

## Editing

- Cut in Final Cut or DaVinci. Color: cool, slightly desaturated. The grow room already has the green; don't push it further.
- The terminal/demo segment (0:55–1:55) must remain a single take. If the model gives a slow or weak response on take 1, re-shoot; do not Frankenstein a "good" answer from multiple takes. This is the verification surface.
- Final export: H.264, 1080p, 60fps, ~30 Mbps. YouTube re-encodes anyway but give it clean source.

## Backup plan if the shoot is rushed

If the timeline collapses, the minimum-viable version is:
1. Single 90-second screen recording of the CLI demo in airplane mode
2. Single 30-second talking-head intro from Michael at the start
3. Static "built on Gemma" card at the end

That's still a valid submission. Don't skip Act 3 (the demo) under any circumstance — it's the only act that can't be faked.
